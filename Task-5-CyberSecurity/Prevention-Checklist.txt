🔒 General Digital Hygiene
Enable Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA): Use 2FA on all academic, social media, and email accounts. This ensures that even if a password is leaked, an attacker cannot gain access without a second form of verification.

Audit Your Digital Footprint: Periodically search for your name online. Remember that digital records are permanent, and small inconsistencies or "faked" skills on profiles can haunt your professional future.

Practice "Strategic Deceleration": Avoid the "Velocity Trap" by slowing down. Taking a few extra seconds to verify a link or a sender’s identity can prevent significant mistakes.

Verify Source Credibility: Before sharing news or academic resources in group chats, check for citations and official references to avoid spreading misinformation.

Secure Your Devices: Ensure your laptop and smartphone have the latest security patches installed. Platforms are responsible for safety, but users must keep their tools updated to be effective.

Be Skeptical of "Urgent" Requests: Scams often succeed by creating a false sense of urgency. If an email or message demands immediate action to avoid a "lockout" or "fine," it is likely a phishing attempt.


💸 Financial & UPI Safety (India Specific)
The "PIN is for Paying" Rule: Never enter your UPI PIN to receive money. Scammers often send "collect requests" disguised as "refunds" or "rewards." Remember: You only need a PIN to send money, never to receive it.

Verify UPI IDs Before Confirming: Always double-check the recipient's name that appears in the app (like GPay, PhonePe, or Paytm) before hitting 'Proceed.' Verify that the name matches the person or business you intend to pay.


🚨 How to Report Cyber Crime
If you encounter a scam, experience financial fraud, or face online harassment, take immediate action through the official government channels:

1.National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal: Visit cybercrime.gov.in to file an online complaint.

2.National Helpline: Call 1930 immediately for financial frauds to increase the chances of freezing the fraudulent transaction.
